Hobbies & Lifestyle

Life beyond the hospital: the habits, places, and pleasures that keep me balanced, inspired, and joyful.

My World Outside Work

Living a Balanced Life

Nursing can be intense, and for that reason I value a lifestyle that restores my energy and keeps me connected to beauty. In my free time, I return to movement, music, literature, photography, and the familiar warmth of Qazvin, the city I call home.

Qazvin, Coffee, and Familiar Streets

I live in Qazvin, Iran, and I feel deeply connected to its historical atmosphere. It is a city with character, memory, and a quiet beauty that reveals itself in old streets, familiar corners, and its many nostalgic coffee shops.

Coffee is one of my small but serious joys. I especially love a latte without cream, and one of my favourite routines is going out for coffee with friends and letting the conversation unfold slowly in a cozy place.

📷

Photography and Nature

I enjoy photography, taking selfies, and capturing natural scenes that feel alive with detail and emotion. When I travel or go camping, taking photographs becomes part of how I remember the day.

🏊

Fitness and Swimming

I keep a regular gym routine, and swimming is one of my favourite activities. It helps reduce work pressure, especially during demanding periods when nursing requires extra emotional and physical energy.

🎸

Music and Persian Literature

I love playing the guitar, especially classical music. I am also very fond of literature, particularly Persian literature and poetry, which offer both beauty and wisdom in a way that stays with me.

🍔

Simple Joys and Warm Company

I enjoy shopping, cheerful conversations, and simple date moments such as having burgers in the car and talking for hours in a comfortable, intimate atmosphere. For me, happiness is often found in ordinary moments shared sincerely.

My Routine

A Day in My Life

Morning

Beginning with Intention

I like to begin the day with calm energy, often with coffee and a few quiet moments before responsibilities begin. A good morning helps everything else feel more grounded.

At Work

Focused Care in the Ward

At the hospital, my attention turns fully toward patient care, teamwork, and clinical responsibility. Even when the shift is intense, I try to remain steady, attentive, and humane.

Evening

Movement, Music, and Recovery

After work, I often recharge through the gym, swimming, music, or reading. These habits help me release pressure and return to myself after demanding clinical days.

Weekends

Friends, Coffee, and Small Adventures

My weekends often include coffee with friends, shopping, camping, taking photographs, and enjoying unhurried conversations in warm and comfortable places.
